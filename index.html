Make Your First
Roblox Game!

A super easy, step-by-step guide that anyone can follow — no coding experience needed! Just click, follow, and you'll have your own game published! 🎉

🚀 Let's Start! 📋 Checklist
🎮
1

🌟 Create Your Roblox Account

First things first — you need a free Roblox account!

📝 How to Sign Up

  1. Open your browser (Chrome, Safari, anything!)
  2. Go to www.roblox.com
  3. You'll see a Sign Up form right on the page
  4. Fill in your Birthday (just pick the correct date)
  5. Choose a fun Username (this is your game developer name!)
  6. Create a strong Password (mix letters + numbers)
  7. Click the big green "Sign Up" button
  8. 🎉 DONE! You now have a Roblox account!
💡 Tip: Remember your username & password! Write it down somewhere safe. You'll need it every time you open Roblox Studio.
2

💜 Download Roblox Studio

This is the FREE app where you'll build your game!

⬇️ Download Steps

  1. Go to www.roblox.com/create
  2. Click "Start Creating"
  3. It will ask you to download Roblox Studio — click Download!
  4. Once downloaded, open the installer file
  5. It installs automatically — just wait a minute ⏳
  6. When it's done, Roblox Studio will open and ask you to log in
  7. Use the account you just created in Step 1!
💡 Tip: Roblox Studio only works on a computer (Windows or Mac). You can't build games on your phone, but people CAN play your game on phones later!
3

💙 Open Studio & Pick a Template

Templates = ready-made starting points so you don't start from zero!

🎨 Choosing Your Template

When Roblox Studio opens, you'll see a screen with lots of template options:

  1. Baseplate — A flat empty world (best for beginners!)
  2. Obby — An obstacle course template
  3. Racing — A car racing game template
  4. Capture The Flag — A team game template

👉 For your first time, click "Baseplate" — it's the simplest!

💡 Tip: Baseplate gives you a big flat green surface. Think of it like an empty playground where you'll place all your cool stuff!

🖥️ What You'll See (The Studio Interface)

Don't panic! Here's what the main areas are:

  • 🔵 The 3D View (center) — This is your game world! You can see your flat baseplate here
  • 📦 Explorer (right side) — Lists everything in your game (like a file manager)
  • 🎨 Properties (right side) — Change colors, sizes, and settings of things you click on
  • 🔧 Toolbar (top) — Buttons to add things, move things, and test your game
💡 Camera Controls: Hold Right Mouse Button + move mouse to look around. Use W/A/S/D keys to fly around your world. Scroll wheel to zoom!
4

💚 Building Your World

Time to add stuff! Let's place blocks (called "Parts") into your game.

🧱 Adding Your First Part

  1. Look at the top toolbar
  2. Find the "Part" button (it has a small cube icon)
  3. Click the little arrow ▼ next to it
  4. Choose a shape: Block, Sphere, Cylinder, or Wedge
  5. A shape appears in your world! 🎉

🔀 Moving, Resizing & Rotating

After placing a part, you can change it using these toolbar buttons:

  • ➡️ Move Tool — Drag the colored arrows to move your part around
  • 📐 Scale Tool — Drag the dots to make it bigger or smaller
  • 🔄 Rotate Tool — Drag the circles to spin it around
💡 Tip: Build a simple tower! Add 3 blocks, stack them, make them different sizes. Congrats — you just built your first structure! 🏗️
5

🌈 Adding Colors & Materials

Let's make your game look pretty!

🎨 Changing Colors

  1. Click on a Part in your game world to select it
  2. Look at the Properties panel on the right side
  3. Find "BrickColor" or "Color"
  4. Click the color box next to it
  5. Pick any color you want! 🌈

✨ Changing Materials

Materials change how the surface looks (shiny, rough, glowing, etc.)

  1. Select your Part
  2. In Properties, find "Material"
  3. Click the dropdown and try:
    • Neon — Makes it glow! ✨
    • Glass — See-through!
    • Wood — Looks like wood planks
    • Grass — Perfect for ground!
    • SmoothPlastic — Clean and shiny
💡 Fun idea: Make a neon-colored dance floor! Place a flat block, make it "Neon" material, give it a bright pink or blue color. Looks amazing! 💃
6

🧸 Adding Cool Models from Toolbox

Why build everything yourself? Grab free ready-made stuff!

📦 Using the Toolbox

  1. Go to the top menu → click View → click Toolbox
  2. A panel opens on the left with a search bar
  3. Search for anything! Try: "house", "car", "tree", "sword"
  4. Click on any model you like — it appears in your game!
  5. Use the Move Tool to place it where you want
⚠️ Be careful: Some models from Toolbox have hidden scripts that might break things. Stick to models with high ratings (thumbs up) and many likes!
7

⚡ Adding Simple Scripts (the magic!)

Scripts make things HAPPEN — don't worry, I'll give you copy-paste code!

🪄 What Are Scripts?

Scripts are little instructions that tell your game what to do. Like a recipe! You don't need to write them from scratch — just copy and paste the ones below. 😊

📝 Example 1: Kill Brick (Touch it = you die!)

Perfect for obby (obstacle course) games:

  1. Add a Part (block) to your game
  2. Make it red so players know it's dangerous!
  3. In the Explorer panel (right side), find your Part
  4. Right-click the Part → Insert Object → Script
  5. A code editor opens. Delete everything in there
  6. Copy-paste this code:
script.Parent.Touched:Connect(function(hit)
  local humanoid = hit.Parent:FindFirstChild("Humanoid")
  if humanoid then
    humanoid.Health = 0
  end
end)
💡 What this does: When a player touches this red block, their character's health goes to 0 (they die and respawn!). That's it!

📝 Example 2: Speed Boost Pad

Step on it = run super fast!

  1. Add a flat Part, color it yellow/green
  2. Right-click → Insert Object → Script
  3. Delete everything, paste this:
script.Parent.Touched:Connect(function(hit)
  local humanoid = hit.Parent:FindFirstChild("Humanoid")
  if humanoid then
    humanoid.WalkSpeed = 50
    wait(5)
    humanoid.WalkSpeed = 16
  end
end)
💡 What this does: Player walks on it → runs fast for 5 seconds → goes back to normal speed. So cool! ⚡

📝 Example 3: A Part That Changes Color

while true do
  script.Parent.BrickColor = BrickColor.Random()
  wait(1)
end
💡 What this does: The block changes to a random color every second! Like a disco light! 🪩
8

🎮 Testing Your Game

Let's see your game in action before publishing!

▶️ How to Test

  1. Look at the top toolbar
  2. Click the big ▶️ Play button
  3. Your character spawns in the game! Walk around, try your kill bricks, speed pads, etc.
  4. When done testing, click the ⏹ Stop button to go back to editing
⚠️ Important: Changes you make WHILE testing (in Play mode) are NOT saved! Always press Stop first, then make your edits.

🐛 Something Not Working?

My script doesn't work! ▼

Check if you pasted the code correctly. Make sure there are no extra spaces or missing letters. Also make sure the Script is inside the Part (drag it in Explorer if needed).

My character falls through the floor! ▼

Your baseplate might have been deleted. Go to Explorer, right-click "Workspace" → Insert Object → Part. Make it really big and flat. Check that CanCollide is turned ON in Properties.

The game looks dark! ▼

In Explorer, find Lighting. Click it. In Properties, set Brightness to 2 or higher. You can also change TimeOfDay to "14:00:00" for afternoon sun!

9

🚀 Publishing Your Game!

THE FINAL STEP! Let's make your game live so anyone in the world can play it! 🌍

📤 How to Publish (Step by Step)

  1. Click File (top-left corner of Studio)
  2. Click "Publish to Roblox"
  3. A window pops up! Fill in:
    • Name — Give your game a cool name!
    • Description — Write what your game is about
    • Genre — Pick a category (Adventure, Obby, etc.)
  4. Click "Create"
  5. Wait for it to upload... ⏳
  6. 🎉 YOUR GAME IS NOW ON ROBLOX!

🌐 Making It Public (So Others Can Play)

By default, your game is private. Let's make it public:

  1. In Roblox Studio, click File → "Game Settings"
  2. Go to the "Permissions" tab
  3. Change it from Private to Public
  4. Click Save
  5. Now ANYONE can find and play your game! 🎊

🔗 Getting Your Game Link

  1. Go to www.roblox.com in your browser
  2. Click your profile icon → "Creations"
  3. You'll see your game listed there!
  4. Click on it → copy the URL from your browser's address bar
  5. Share that link with friends! 🥳
💡 Pro tip: After publishing, you can always update your game! Just make changes in Studio, then go to File → "Publish to Roblox" again. It updates the same game!
